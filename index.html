AlphaUnlock FRP Bypass (Android 5.1 – 10)

This method provides a universal way to bypass Factory Reset Protection (FRP) on most Android devices running Android 5.1 through 10. This guide is intended for legitimate use only—such as recovering access to your own locked device.

Steps to Bypass FRP:

  1. On the Terms of Service screen after a factory reset, find and tap a mailto: link (usually found in license or help text).
  2. This opens the Gmail app. Tap the "Learn more about adding an account" link.
  3. From the Google Help page that opens, tap the Share button.
  4. Select Google Go or another browser that allows URL intents.
  5. Navigate to frpbypass.io.
  6. Tap an intent link to open Settings, such as:
    intent://com.android.settings/#Intent;scheme=android-app;end
  7. Once in Settings, go to Security → Screen lock and set a new PIN, password, or pattern.
  8. Restart or reset the phone again via the system menu.
  9. After rebooting, the device should now prompt for the new lock code instead of the previous Google account.

Important Notes

  • This method is most effective on devices running Android 5.1 to 10.
  • Android 11 and newer have patched most of these bypass methods.
  • Ensure Wi-Fi is connected and, if necessary, insert a SIM card to enable Gmail and sharing functions.

Use this method responsibly and only on devices you own or are authorized to unlock.
